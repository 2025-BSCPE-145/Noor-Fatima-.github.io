My Journey in Programming Fundamentals

One of the most interesting courses during my first year of Computer Engineering was Programming Fundamentals. Before joining university, I had heard that programming was difficult and required a lot of practice. I was both excited and nervous because this was my first formal introduction to the world of coding.

In the beginning, even simple concepts such as variables, data types, and input-output statements seemed new and confusing. Writing my first program and seeing it run successfully gave me a feeling of achievement. It was a small program, but it made me realize that programming is not only about writing code; it is about solving problems in a logical way.

As the semester progressed, we learned about decision structures, loops, arrays, functions, and other important programming concepts. Every new topic felt like a new challenge. Sometimes my programs produced errors that took hours to solve. There were moments when I became frustrated because I could not understand why my code was not working. However, every mistake taught me something new.

One of the biggest lessons I learned from Programming Fundamentals was the importance of practice. Programming cannot be learned only by reading books or watching lectures. It requires writing code again and again until the concepts become clear. The more programs I wrote, the more confident I became in my abilities.

I also learned the importance of debugging. Sometimes a program would fail because of a missing semicolon, a wrong condition, or a small typing mistake. At first these errors seemed annoying, but later I understood that debugging is an essential part of programming. It teaches patience, attention to detail, and problem-solving skills.

Dr. Bilal Ahmad, who taught us Programming Fundamentals, always encouraged us to think beyond memorizing syntax. He emphasized understanding the logic behind every program. Through his guidance, I realized that programming is a way of thinking. It teaches us how to analyze problems and find efficient solutions.

Another interesting aspect of the course was learning how programming can be applied to real-world problems. Dr. Bilal Ahmad often discussed Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning applications and how programming forms the foundation of these advanced technologies. These discussions motivated me to improve my programming skills and explore new areas of computer science.

Programming Fundamentals also improved my confidence. At the start of the semester, writing even a small program felt difficult. By the end of the course, I was able to solve more complex problems and understand how different concepts work together. This progress showed me that learning is a gradual process and that consistent effort leads to success.

The course taught me valuable lessons that go beyond coding. It improved my logical thinking, patience, and ability to solve problems systematically. These skills are not only useful in programming but also in everyday life and future engineering projects.

Looking back, Programming Fundamentals was one of the most important courses in my academic journey. It laid the foundation for future courses and introduced me to the world of software development. I know that there is still a lot to learn, but this course gave me the confidence and motivation to continue improving my skills and exploring new technologies.
