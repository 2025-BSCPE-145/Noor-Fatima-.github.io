What I Learned from Database Systems

Database Systems was one of the most interesting and practical courses that I studied during my Computer Engineering degree. Before taking this course, I used to think that data was simply information stored inside files. However, this course completely changed my understanding and showed me how important databases are in modern technology.

Almost every application that we use today depends on a database. Social media platforms, banking systems, online shopping websites, hospitals, and universities all rely on databases to store and manage information efficiently. Learning this made me realize that databases are one of the foundations of computer science and software engineering.

At the beginning of the course, we learned about the basic concepts of databases and database management systems. I was introduced to tables, rows, columns, primary keys, and relationships between different entities. These concepts seemed simple at first, but they became very important when we started designing our own databases.

One of my favorite topics was the Entity Relationship (ER) Diagram. Designing ER diagrams taught me how to analyze a real-world problem and convert it into a structured database model. It was interesting to see how entities, attributes, and relationships could represent complex systems in an organized way.

Another important topic was normalization. At first, normalization looked difficult because there were many rules and forms to remember. After practicing several examples, I understood its purpose. Normalization helps reduce redundancy, improve data consistency, and make databases more efficient. It showed me the importance of good database design.

The most exciting part of the course was learning SQL, which stands for Structured Query Language. Writing SQL queries made me feel like I was communicating directly with the database. I learned how to create tables, insert data, update records, delete information, and retrieve specific data using different queries.

I particularly enjoyed writing SELECT queries because they allowed me to search for information in different ways. Joining tables and applying conditions made me appreciate how powerful databases can be. Every new query felt like solving a small puzzle.

Dr. Bilal Ahmad taught us Database Systems in a way that connected theory with practical applications. He often discussed how high-quality data is essential for Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning models. He emphasized that good datasets, especially medical datasets, are extremely valuable because they are collected with precision and accuracy. This perspective helped me understand that databases are not just about storing data; they are also about providing reliable information for solving real-world problems.

Through this course, I also learned the importance of planning before implementation. A poorly designed database can create many problems in the future, while a well-designed database improves efficiency and reliability. This lesson is valuable not only in database design but also in many other areas of engineering and software development.

Looking back, Database Systems has become one of my favorite subjects because it combines logic, problem-solving, and practical applications. The knowledge gained from this course will be useful in future projects and advanced technologies such as Artificial Intelligence, Data Science, and software development.

This course taught me that data is one of the most valuable resources in the modern world. Understanding how to organize, manage, and analyze data is an essential skill for every engineer. I am excited to continue learning more about databases and apply these concepts in future projects and real-world applications.
